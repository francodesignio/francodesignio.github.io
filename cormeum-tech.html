Cormeum was a start-up made up of a team of eight engineering students, including myself, whose purpose was to provide an innovative alternative to the evaluation process of drugs under development.

Who we are

At Cormeum Technologies, we have developed a multi-organ on a chip device capable of conducting cytotoxicity tests in the preclinical phase of drugs, giving insight on the systemic level using human tissue. Our technology consists of a platform that houses different types of cells and puts them in contact with the drug being evaluated, performing a systematic simulation of cell activity in its true physiological environment. This way we provide a new way of accelerating the testing process to determine whether a drug can continue in development.

Physiological Simulation

We provide a 3D cell growth environment that simulates the conditions that cells experience within the human body.

Automation

We have an automated system that considerably reduces human error.

Microfluidics

We use microfluidic technology, that is, we work on a micro scale. This means that we use considerably less reagents, reducing the impact on the environment.
